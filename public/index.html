Under the hood

How it works

No remote code, no accounts — just a content script that runs on youtube.com and keeps your blocklist on-device.

1

Block in one click

The 🚫 button on each video — and on channel pages — captures the channel's @handle or /channel/ id and saves it locally.

2

Hides before paint

A stylesheet injected at document_start hides cards whose channel link matches your blocklist, so they never flash in.

3

Catches the rest

A light DOM sweep handles cards without a channel link and the Shorts shelf, matching by handle, id or name.

4

Survives the SPA

A MutationObserver plus navigation hooks re-apply on every in-app route change, so blocks hold as you browse.

One minute, any Chromium browser

Install it

Works in Chrome, Edge, Brave and any Chromium browser. It won't be on the Chrome Web Store (why?) — you load it unpacked, which takes about a minute and lets you read every line of the open source first.

1

Download & unzip

Grab channelblock.zip and extract it anywhere.

2

Open extensions

Go to chrome://extensions and turn on Developer mode (top right).

3

Load unpacked

Click Load unpacked and select the unzipped channelblock folder.

4

Open YouTube

Reload any YouTube tab and hover a video — the block button is right there.

Using Firefox? Grab the signed-build XPI — an AMO listing is on the way.

Questions

Good to know

How is this different from “Don't recommend channel”?

YouTube's option is a soft signal — it nudges the algorithm, which routinely surfaces the channel again within days. ChannelBlock is a hard, deterministic filter: a blocked channel is removed on every page load, across home, search, recommendations and Shorts, until you unblock it.

What data does it collect?

Your blocklist stays on your device. The only thing it ever sends is an anonymous, aggregate, opt-out snapshot about once per browsing session: the version, which toggles are on, and two counters (channels blocked, videos hidden). No account, no identifier, no IP stored, no URLs, never your browsing. Turn off “Share anonymous stats” and nothing is sent. Every number we hold is public at /api/stats.

Is it really free? What's the catch?

It's free, no catch and no subscription. Cross-device blocklist sync may arrive later as an optional paid extra, but the core blocking is never gated.

Why isn't it on the Chrome Web Store?

An extension that hides channels cuts against YouTube's own product, so we don't expect a smooth review. ChannelBlock ships as an unpacked install from the zip (which also lets you read every line first). It's tiny, MIT-licensed and the full source is on GitHub.

Is there a Firefox version?

Yes — grab the XPI. Since Mozilla's policies are friendlier to this kind of tool, it may also land on addons.mozilla.org as a one-click install.

Can I move my blocklist between browsers?

Yes. Use Export in the popup to save your blocklist as a JSON file, then Import it in another browser or profile. You can also paste a channel URL or @handle to block without visiting it.

A blocked channel still slips through — what do I do?

YouTube changes its markup often. ChannelBlock matches channels by their handle and id rather than fragile labels, and a watchdog re-applies after navigation. If something new appears, reload the tab from the popup; updates ship to keep coverage complete.
