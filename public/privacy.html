Privacy

Last updated: June 2026

The short version: your blocklist never leaves your device. ChannelBlock has no account, logs no IP, and never sees your browsing. The only thing it can send is an anonymous, aggregate, opt-out usage count — and you can turn that off in one click.

What stays on your device

Your blocked-channels list, your toggle settings and your chosen language are stored locally with the browser's extension storage (chrome.storage.local). They are never uploaded. Use Export / Import in the popup to move the list between your own browsers — it's a plain JSON file you control.

What it sends (and only if you let it)

If "Share anonymous stats" is on (default), roughly once per browsing session the extension sends a single snapshot to channelblock.zlef.fr/api/collect containing:

  • the extension version;
  • which feature toggles are on (e.g. block Shorts, guard pages) — booleans only;
  • two counters: how many channels you've blocked and how many videos were hidden.

That's the complete payload. It does not include any account or identifier, your IP is not stored, and it never contains channel names, URLs, watch history or anything about what you browse. The numbers are aggregated per day and published openly at /api/stats.

Turning it off

Open the popup and switch off "Share anonymous stats". Nothing is sent from then on, and any pending counters are dropped.

Permissions

  • storage — to save your blocklist and settings locally.
  • *://*.youtube.com/* — so the content script can filter your feed on YouTube. It runs nowhere else.
  • channelblock.zlef.fr — the destination for the optional anonymous stats ping only.

No third parties

There are no ads, no analytics SDKs, no trackers, and no data is sold or shared. ChannelBlock is open source (MIT) — you can read exactly what it does on GitHub.
